About This Simulation

NGSS HS-ETS1-1: Analyze a major global challenge to specify qualitative and quantitative criteria and constraints for solutions that account for societal needs and wants.

In this simulation, you act as a city planner addressing a major global challenge: securing a reliable clean water supply for a growing population.

Instructions:

  1. Define Constraints: First, you must explicitly set the quantitative boundaries for your project (budget limit, environmental impact limit) and the criteria for success (target water capacity).
  2. Propose Solutions: Once constraints are set, choose from various engineering and social solutions. Each has trade-offs regarding cost, water yield, environmental damage, and public approval.
  3. Analyze Impacts: Advance time year by year to see how your choices affect the city. Try to meet your target water capacity without violating your budget or environmental constraints.
