Evolving Wings of Nebraska's Cliff Swallows

HS-LS4-4: Natural Selection & Adaptation

For thousands of years, cliff swallows nested on cliff faces, where longer wings were advantageous for energy-efficient gliding. However, as humans built highways and bridges, many swallows adapted to nesting under these structures. In this simulation, investigate how the introduction of high-speed traffic alters the selective pressures on wing span, favoring maneuverability (shorter wings) over gliding efficiency (longer wings).

Environment Controls

Controls the frequency of cars. Higher traffic requires greater maneuverability to survive.
