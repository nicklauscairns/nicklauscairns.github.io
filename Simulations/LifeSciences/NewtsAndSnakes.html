The Co-Evolutionary Arms Race

HS-LS4-5: Environmental Changes & Species Evolution

The rough-skinned newt produces tetrodotoxin (TTX), one of the deadliest neurotoxins on Earth. In certain geographic hotspots, local common garter snakes have evolved resistance to this poison, sparking an extreme evolutionary "arms race" where newts become ever more toxic and snakes ever more resistant. Investigate how this predator-prey dynamic acts as an environmental change that drives rapid, localized evolution.
