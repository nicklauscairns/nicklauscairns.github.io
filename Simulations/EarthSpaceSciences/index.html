NGSS High School Earth and Space Sciences Standards Aligned Content

Simulations

HS-ESS1-1

Develop a model based on evidence to illustrate the life span of the sun and the role of nuclear fusion in the sun’s core to release energy in the form of radiation.

  • Stellar Phenomena Simulator - An interactive simulation exploring the stages of stellar life, from a stellar nursery to a star's ultimate fate. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS1-2

Construct an explanation of the Big Bang theory based on astronomical evidence of light spectra, motion of distant galaxies, and composition of matter in the universe.

  • Big Bang Evidence Explorer - An interactive exploration of the three key pieces of evidence supporting the Big Bang Theory: cosmic redshift, primordial composition, and the cosmic microwave background. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS1-3

Communicate scientific ideas about the way stars, over their life cycle, produce elements.

  • Stellar Phenomena Simulator - An interactive simulation exploring the stages of stellar life, from a stellar nursery to a star's ultimate fate. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS1-4

Use mathematical or computational representations to predict the motion of orbiting objects in the solar system.

  • Orbital Motion & Kepler's Laws Simulation - An interactive simulation exploring Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation and Kepler's Laws by adjusting planetary parameters. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]
  • Orbital Motion & Kepler's Third Law - An interactive simulation exploring how star mass, initial distance, and velocity affect a planet's orbit, and demonstrating Kepler's Third Law. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS1-5

Evaluate evidence of the past and current movements of continental and oceanic crust and the theory of plate tectonics to explain the ages of crustal rocks.

  • Radiometric Dating Explorer - An interactive tool simulating radioactive decay to demonstrate how scientists determine the absolute ages of crustal rocks and meteorites. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS1-6

Apply scientific reasoning and evidence from ancient Earth materials, meteorites, and other planetary surfaces to construct an account of Earth’s formation and early history.

  • Radiometric Dating Explorer - An interactive tool simulating radioactive decay to demonstrate how scientists determine the absolute ages of crustal rocks and meteorites. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-1

Develop a model to illustrate how Earth’s internal and surface processes operate at different spatial and temporal scales to form continental and ocean-floor features.

  • Tectonic Landscape Modeler - An interactive simulation exploring how slow constructive forces (uplift) and destructive forces (weathering, mass wasting) shape Earth's surface over geologic time. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-2

Analyze geoscience data to make the claim that one change to Earth's surface can create feedbacks that cause changes to other Earth systems.

  • Ice-Albedo Feedback Loop - An interactive simulation demonstrating the positive feedback loop between rising global temperatures, melting sea ice, and decreasing surface albedo. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-3

Develop a model based on evidence of Earth’s interior to describe the cycling of matter by thermal convection.

  • Mantle Convection Explorer - An interactive simulation exploring how the outward flow of energy from Earth's core drives thermal convection in the mantle and the surface expression of plate tectonics. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-4

Use a model to describe how variations in the flow of energy into and out of Earth’s systems result in changes in climate.

  • Greenhouse Effect & Earth's Energy Budget Simulation - An interactive simulation exploring how greenhouse gases affect Earth's energy budget and global temperature. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-5

Plan and conduct an investigation of the properties of water and its effects on Earth materials and surface processes.

  • Water Properties & Earth Processes - An interactive lab environment exploring frost wedging, chemical weathering, and stream transport. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-6

Develop a quantitative model to describe the cycling of carbon among the hydrosphere, atmosphere, geosphere, and biosphere.

  • Global Carbon Cycle Model - A quantitative box-model simulation demonstrating the conservation of mass and the flows of carbon between Earth's major reservoirs, highlighting human impacts. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS2-7

Construct an argument based on evidence about the simultaneous coevolution of Earth’s systems and life on Earth.

  • The Great Oxidation Event - An interactive timeline model simulating how early photosynthetic life altered Earth's atmosphere, exhausted ocean chemical sinks, and paved the way for complex aerobic life. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-1

Construct an explanation based on evidence for how the availability of natural resources, occurrence of natural hazards, and changes in climate have influenced human activity.

  • Human Settlement & Migration Simulator - An interactive map demonstrating how human populations migrate towards natural resources and are displaced by natural hazards and climate change. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-2

Evaluate competing design solutions for developing, managing, and utilizing energy and mineral resources based on cost-benefit ratios.*

  • Sustainable Resource Management Simulator - A computational city-builder simulation modeling the cost-benefit ratios of different energy and land management policies. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-3

Create a computational simulation to illustrate the relationships among management of natural resources, the sustainability of human populations, and biodiversity.

  • Sustainable Resource Management Simulator - A computational simulation where users must balance human population growth against biodiversity loss and resource depletion. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-4

Evaluate or refine a technological solution that reduces impacts of human activities on natural systems.*

  • Global Climate Impacts & Mitigation Forecast - A computational dashboard allowing users to evaluate different technological solutions (like carbon capture or geoengineering) to mitigate climate impacts. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-5

Analyze geoscience data and the results from global climate models to make an evidence-based forecast of the current rate of global or regional climate change and associated future impacts to Earth systems.

  • Global Climate Impacts & Mitigation Forecast - A computational dashboard forecasting the future rate of climate change and its specific impacts on glacial ice and ocean pH. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]

HS-ESS3-6

Use a computational representation to illustrate the relationships among Earth systems and how those relationships are being modified due to human activity.

  • Global Climate Impacts & Mitigation Forecast - A computational representation showing how human activity modifies the atmosphere, which in turn modifies the hydrosphere and cryosphere. [2026-03-12 12:00:00]
