Wave-Particle Duality

Evaluate evidence demonstrating that electromagnetic radiation acts as both a wave and a particle depending on the phenomenon (HS-PS4-3).

Double-Slit Experiment

When light passes through two narrow slits, it creates a pattern on the back wall.

The Argument for Waves:

If light were purely a stream of particles (like bullets), we would expect exactly two bright lines on the back wall corresponding to the two slits.

Instead, we see an interference pattern (multiple stripes).

This proves light travels as a wave, diffracting out of the slits and adding/canceling (constructive and destructive interference) before hitting the wall.

Photoelectric Effect

Shining light on a metal plate. Can the light knock electrons off the metal?

Low Energy (Red) Green High Energy (Violet)

The Argument for Particles:

If light were purely a continuous wave, shining a bright red light for a long time should eventually build up enough energy to knock off an electron.

However, red light NEVER ejects electrons, no matter how bright or how long you wait. But dim blue light ejects them instantly.

This proves light travels in discrete packets (photons). A single red photon doesn't have enough energy to overcome the threshold (work function), while a single blue photon does.
